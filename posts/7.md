Okay, let's talk video options. Because everyone needs video, right?

Been thinking a lot about how SaaS companies get their product demos made. You basically have 4 choices:

1.  Agency: Big budget. Big headaches. $20k+ for one video? Seriously? And dealing with layers of project managers? No thanks. Unless you're swimming in VC money and have zero time... maybe?

2.  Freelancer: Cheaper than an agency. But finding a *good* one who understands SaaS *and* video production? Good luck. Plus, you're still managing the whole thing. My time is worth something, you know?

3.  DIY: Screen recording + iMovie. We've all been there. Ends up looking… well, DIY. Okay for internal comms, maybe. Def not for your landing page.

4. Remawt: OK, yeah, I'm biased. But seriously: studio-quality demo videos, AI-powered, in *minutes*, not weeks. Costs less than hiring a freelancer for a day. No, it's not perfect for *everything* – like custom brand films or crazy animations. But for showing off your software? Pretty damn good.

My two cents.
